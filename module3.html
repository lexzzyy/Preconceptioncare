Reproductive Life Plan

A reproductive life plan is a set of personal goals about having (or not having) children, accompanied by family planning intended to help achieve those goals. Reproductive life plans reduce the risk of unintended pregnancy and increase the number of women who plan their pregnancies, and engage in healthy behaviours before becoming pregnant. The CDC recommends that everyone make a reproductive life plan based on personal values and resources.

Well-woman visits

American College of Obstetricians and Gynaecologists (ACOG) recommends annual assessments to counsel women about preventive care and to provide recommended services such as screening and immunizations. A number of illnesses can be prevented when proper well-woman care is a priority, and even illnesses that can’t be prevented have a much better prognosis when detected early. In addition, a woman who has been receiving annual well-woman care is more likely to be in optimal health before any subsequent pregnancy. Increase proportion of Healthy Start women participants that receive a well-woman visit to 80%.

Recommendations for implementation of PCC

Frequently asked questions

Q. What is PCC?

Preconception Care is an organized and comprehensive program of health care that identifies and reduces a woman's reproductive risks before conception through risk assessment, health promotion, and interventions.

Q. Are preconception vitamins necessary?

It is important to start taking a preconception vitamin as soon as you attempt trying to conceive. Taking a high-quality preconception vitamin, along with eating a balanced diet, it helps prepare your body for conception and a healthy pregnancy.

Q. Should my husband come to my preconception appointment?

Absolutely, Yes he will learn what is necessary for a healthy pregnancy, what to expect and what to avoid at the same time it shows the level of partner’s support.

Q. What is Body mass index?

Body mass index is a simple calculation using a person's height and weight. The formula is BMI = kg/m2 where kg is a person's weight in kilograms and m2 is height in metres squared. A BMI of 25.0 or more is overweight, while the healthy range is 18.5 to 24.9.

FAQs continue

Q. When should I make a preconception appointment?

One of the first steps you should take is to get preconception care from your doctor /midwife/nurse at least 3 months before you become pregnant. It will improve your chance of conceiving, reduce the risk of birth defects, and get you as ready as possible for a healthy pregnancy and baby.

Q. What if, I am not planning to get pregnancy?

You can embark on family planning if you are sexually active. Meet your midwife to discuss your family planning needs.

Q. What test should I do before getting pregnant?

A number of tests such as premarital genotype screening, blood grouping, Rhesus factor screening, HIV testing, and Hepatitis B to detect any conditions that could affect pregnancy or chances of conceiving.

Q. Will I experience any pain after taking Tetanus Toxoid vaccine?

As with any other injection, TT injection is associated with slight bearable pain that wears off after some times. It is usually given at the Left upper arm.
